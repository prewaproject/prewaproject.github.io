About the Project

Why Phosphorus Matters

Phosphorus is irreplaceable — critical for food production, cell function, and ecosystem health. Yet the European Union depends almost entirely on imports, and price volatility has highlighted the fragility of this supply chain. The EU has classified phosphorus as a Critical Raw Material.

Project Objectives
  • ✓
    Determine optimal SMC & SS mixture ratios for P-rich ash production via fluidized bed combustion.
  • ✓
    Develop optimized P recovery technology using microwave heating and biocarbon reducing agents.
  • ✓
    Evaluate environmental impacts of energy generation and biofuel alternatives through Life Cycle Assessment (LCA).
  • ✓
    Apply circular economy approaches for waste management and resource recovery at scale.
Origin: the PHIGO Project PREWA builds on findings from the PHIGO project. Although sewage sludge is recognized for high phosphorus content, PHIGO compared it with chicken manure — and subsequent work pivoted toward sewage sludge & spent mushroom compost as the most promising combined feedstock.
Project Originality
  • ★
    First study to combine fluidized bed combustion and microwave heating for P recovery from SMC + SS mixtures.
  • ★
    Comprehensive Life Cycle Assessment comparing clean P recovery vs. alternative biochar production pathways.
  • ★
    Targets advancement from TRL4 → TRL5, establishing a foundation for future commercial applications.
